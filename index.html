1950's

a gift from the heart

Following World War II, a Danish baker named Thomas Dam faced economic hardships. The glorious world of memes had not reached him yet and so his bags were empty. With a heavy heart and his daughter’s birthday around the corner, Dam crafted a cute wooden troll doll inspired by Scandinavian folklore. Little did he know, he had just sparked a revolution.

The Mysterious Painting

The little troll sprang to life and peered around the workshop. Tidy and clean with the fresh smell of saw dust and wood chips in the air, something caught his eye. A glorious aged painting, captured his gaze. But what could it be?
early
1960's

Trade routes are planned

The troll continued to wander around and realised he was ass out butt-naked. He needed some shorts. On the hunt for thread and some scraps, this very capable troll stumbled upon a map. This map did not only depict trade routes between Denmark and the rest of Europe, but all the way to the USA!

Mass production was underway

Thomas Dam's troll dolls quickly gained popularity and soon mass production was underway! The little troll could not believe how quickly the other trolls were made. Throughout the excitement, the memory of the strange painting on his makers wall would not fade.

International Markets

By 1961, Dam's troll dolls had reached international markets including the United States and the little troll followed them all the way. Front and centre the trolls were taking the toy market by storm!
mid
1960's

Dam Fights

As troll dolls joined children all over the world in their homes, Thomas Dam’s company was struggling to hold onto their original troll designs and soon numerous imitations began to flood the market. His first ever troll was caught in the cross fire, torn between adventure and returning home to Denmark with Dam.

The troll decides

The little troll had decided. Adventure! Joining the other trolls eagerly awaiting a new home. Shelves were stocked all over the country and they were flying off!

A new home!

Everything was looking up for the little troll, from one continent to the next, his adventure was well underway. Children seemed to love their new dolls. The faded painting a thought of the past as a new life of love and affection clouded reality.
mid
70-80's

The trolls got tossed

Unfortunately the troll hype had started to wane. The little children lost interest in their colourful friends. If only they had held onto them. As the little troll sat in a dustbin contemplating where it all went wrong, the distant memory of a faded painting returned.

The troll adventure continues

What could it be? What was that faded painting on Thomas Dam’s wall? The little troll wandered the streets as these thoughts tumbled through its mind, the adventure was not over.

The real story begins here and you are an important part of it. The little troll had found its destiny. The brightly illuminated billboards felt like home. The characters displayed like the family it was always meant to have.
